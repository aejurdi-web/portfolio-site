## Thesis

Supermicro is a genuine AI infrastructure growth story, not merely riding the rising tide of hyperscaler capex. But the market is no longer mispricing it the way a typical undervaluation thesis would require: at $33.91 and a roughly $22B market cap, the stock trades around 12x-14x EV/EBITDA and 18x-25x forward P/E, landing squarely in line with peers like Dell, NetApp, and HPE rather than at a discount. TTM EBITDA of roughly $1.6B-$1.8B implies a thin ~6% EBITDA margin, reflecting the company's traditionally low-margin server integration model even as AI-driven demand accelerates. In June 2026, Supermicro announced a $39B order backlog alongside a $7B equity raise — a combination that signals real demand but also dilution and execution risk through FY2027. A 52-week range of $19.48 to $62.36, paired with a beta of 2.58, shows how violently the market has already re-rated this name in both directions. Compounding that volatility, two distinct legal threads run through the stock: a 2024-25 accounting and auditor crisis that is now resolved, and a separate, unresolved March 2026 DOJ indictment of a co-founder for alleged export-control violations, detailed below.

## Legal & Governance Overhang

<Callout>
The 2024-25 accounting and auditor crisis — which included a delayed 10-K, the resignation of Supermicro's auditor, and a period of Nasdaq listing non-compliance — is resolved. The company regained Nasdaq compliance in January 2026.

Separately, and unresolved as of this writing: in March 2026, the DOJ indicted co-founder and board member Yih-Shyan Liaw, along with two others, on charges related to alleged export-control violations and chip smuggling to China. A related securities fraud class action against the company remains ongoing. Neither matter is reflected in a clean resolution, and both carry binary, stock-moving risk independent of the underlying business trajectory.
</Callout>

## Catalysts

$39B order backlog execution through FY2027. NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL4 platform ramp. Potential resolution of the legal overhang.

## Risks

Continued margin compression from competitive "price war" dynamics among server OEMs. Dilution from the $7B equity raise. Extreme volatility, reflected in a 2.58 beta and a 52-week range spanning $19.48 to $62.36. Unresolved legal exposure tied to the DOJ indictment of a co-founder and the related securities litigation.

## Status

This is an active position, underwritten on an 18-24 month thesis horizon rather than a near-term catalyst trade. The core call — that Supermicro's AI infrastructure growth is real and continuing — is intact, but position sizing and conviction are calibrated to the unresolved legal overhang, which could move the stock sharply in either direction independent of the underlying business trajectory.
